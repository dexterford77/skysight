FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Q: What makes the Skysight® special?

A: Our patented tri-tinted pattern uses different tints to balance the light for three viewing regions:

  • dark tint for outside
  • light tint for the instruments
  • moderate tint for reading material

Q: How Can Skysight sunglasses help me?

A: The Skysight tint pattern helps viewing in the following conditions:

  • Extreme contrast
  • Glare
  • Stark visual environments

Q: What is the contrast problem?

A: The eyes adapt to the brighter light from outside or from reading material so the dimmer instrument panel is hard to see.

Q: What causes glare?

A: The lens of the eye creates an illumination when it scatters bright light from outside or from reading material. This illumination makes the dimmer instruments difficult to see.

Q: Why Do Skysight sunglasses work better than regular sunglasses?

A: Conventional (regular) sunglasses have uniformly tinted lenses that evenly reduce light from outside and inside the aircraft.

  • The dark tint required to reduce the outside brightness can be too dark for the instrument panel
  • The Skysight lens is separated into three different viewing regions with three unique tint densities:
    • Outside (dark)
    • the instrument panel (light)
    • reading material (moderate)

Q: Why Do Skysight sunglasses work better than gradient sunglasses?

A: Gradient sunglasses are tinted dark at the top and light at the bottom.

  • Gradient sunglasses do not compensate for the sharp change in brightness between the outside light and instrument panel.
  • Gradient sunglasses do little for glare because they allow too much outside light to enter the pilot's eyes when viewing the instrument panel.
  • Pilots often find gradient sunglasses
    • not dark enough for outside light
    • too dark for the instruments
    • not dark enough for reading material
  • Skysight lenses overcome contrast and reduce glare by having dark tint in all areas except those areas used to view the instrument panel and reading material.
